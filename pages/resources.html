Course Topics

The IB Physics syllabus is organized into core topics.

Unit A

Space, Time, and Motion

Explores how objects move and interact through Newton's laws, covering force, energy, momentum, and the limits of classical theory at high speeds and small scales.

Unit B

Particulate Nature of Matter

Studies matter as particles in motion to explain heat, gas behavior, thermodynamic limits, and how particle behavior connects to electric circuits.

Unit C

Wave Behavior

Investigates how waves, from sound to light, arise from oscillations, focusing on interference as the defining feature of wave behavior and the doorway to quantum theory.

Unit D

Fields

Examines how masses and charges create gravitational and electric fields that act across space, culminating in Maxwell's unification of electromagnetism and Einstein's geometric view of gravity.

Unit E

Quantum and Nuclear Physics

Explores the discrete, probabilistic world of atoms and nuclei: wave-particle duality, radioactivity, nuclear forces, fission, and fusion, and their connection to the origin of the universe.
