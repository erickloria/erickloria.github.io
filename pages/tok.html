Argument Scaffold

Once the prompt is dissected, move into a clear claim-argument-example structure and justify how each object contributes to answering the selected prompt.

Claim expand_more
Start with a direct answer to the prompt in one clear sentence so the commentary has a visible line of reasoning from the start.
Argument expand_more
Build reasoning grounded in TOK concepts, Areas of Knowledge, methods, and logic rather than only summary or description.
Example expand_more
Use a concrete case that shows the claim working in context, then align that example to one of the three exhibition objects.

Scaffolding Perspectives

Strong TOK writing acknowledges alternative perspectives, responds to counterclaims, and reflects on the assumptions that shape the final position.

Counterclaim

Recognize the strength of a reasonable counterclaim before responding to it. That usually makes the final position more credible.

Reflection

Show self-awareness by identifying an assumption, bias, or perspective that complicated your original view.
